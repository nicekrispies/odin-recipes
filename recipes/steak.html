Pan Seared Steak

Everybody loves a good steak. A thick, juicy hunk of meat cooked to their desired doneness? Who can resist that? I surely can't! In this recipe, I will illustrate how I prepare my steaks.

Ingredients

  • A steak of your choice
  • A high smoke point oil
  • Meat thermometer
  • Salt & pepper
  • Butter, garlic, and thyme (optional)

Steps

  1. Take your steak out and season all over with salt and pepper.
  2. Place your steak back into the fridge for 1 day or overnight to allow the salt to diffuse.
  3. Take your steak back out and pat it dry.
  4. Heat the oil in a cast iron or pan until it's just about to smoke.
  5. Add the steak making sure that you are laying it away from yourself.
  6. Let the steak sit on one side for 3 to 4 minutes before flipping.
  7. Flip the steak and lower the heat if you're adding aromatics and butter.
  8. Optional: Add butter and aromatics. Use a spoon to baste the steak in the melted butter.
  9. Stick your meat thermometer in sideways and cook until you steak is about 5 degrees out from your desired temperature.
  10. Take steak out of the pan and let it rest for a few minutes.
